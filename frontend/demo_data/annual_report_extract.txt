QUALIFIED OPINION

We have audited the financial statements of Ramesh Steel & Fabrications Private
Limited ("the Company"), which comprise the Balance Sheet as at 31st March 2023,
the Statement of Profit and Loss, Statement of Cash Flows, and the Statement of
Changes in Equity for the year then ended.

In our opinion, except for the matters described in the Basis for Qualified Opinion
paragraph below, the aforesaid standalone financial statements give a true and fair
view in conformity with Indian Accounting Standards (Ind AS).

BASIS FOR QUALIFIED OPINION

1. Going Concern – Stretched Working Capital:
   As detailed in Note 28, the Company's net current assets declined from INR 3.82
   Crore (FY 2021-22) to INR 2.96 Crore (FY 2022-23). The Working Capital Turnover
   ratio has deteriorated, and the Company is heavily reliant on short-term bank
   borrowings to fund operations. As at 31st March 2023, outstanding overdue trade
   payables aged beyond 90 days stood at INR 1.23 Crore. Management has represented
   that steps are being taken to restructure working capital facilities, but no formal
   restructuring approval was obtained as at the date of this report.
   In view of the above, there exists material uncertainty that may cast significant
   doubt on the Company's ability to continue as a going concern.

2. Related Party Transactions:
   During the year, the Company entered into transactions aggregating INR 12.45 Crore
   with Sus Metals & Alloys Private Ltd (a related entity by virtue of common
   directorship by the promoter Shri Ramesh Kumar Sharma) and INR 8.70 Crore with
   Ashwin Trading Company (in which a promoter family member holds directorship).
   The Company has not obtained independent valuation or arm's length certification
   for these transactions during the year. These are, however, disclosed as related
   party transactions as required under IND AS 24.

3. Revenue Recognition:
   Certain invoices totalling INR 3.48 Crore raised during Q4 FY 2022-23 to
   Sus Metals & Alloys Pvt Ltd and Padmavati Distributors LLP have not been
   confirmed for receipt by the counter-parties as at the balance sheet date.
   We were unable to independently confirm realisation from these parties.
   This is a matter of significant concern from an audit perspective.

KEY AUDIT MATTERS

Revenue from Operations – Completeness and Timing of Recognition
The Company recognised revenue of INR 48.52 Crore during the year. We noted that
revenue concentration with a limited number of related parties increased during the
year. 26.3% of total revenue is attributable to Sus Metals & Alloys Pvt Ltd and
Ashwin Trading Company, both of which are related parties. The timing of recognition
of certain invoices near the year-end is identified as a key audit matter.

SECTION 4: DEBT RECOVERY TRIBUNAL
───────────────────────────────────

NOTE 29 – Legal Proceedings

The Company has received a notice from the Debt Recovery Tribunal (DRT),
Mumbai Bench under Section 19 of the Recovery of Debts and Bankruptcy Act, 1993
filed by Punjab National Bank. The notice pertains to outstanding dues on a
term loan facility that was classified as NPA (Non-Performing Asset) by
Punjab National Bank in FY 2021-22, with outstanding principal and interest
of INR 1,42,75,000 as on the date of filing.

The Company has settled a significant portion of this outstanding and entered
into a settlement (OTS) for the balance. As at 31-Mar-2023, the residual amount
of INR 24,50,000 is under negotiation. The legal notice from DRT has been
acknowledged. The promoter has provided a personal guarantee for settlement.
